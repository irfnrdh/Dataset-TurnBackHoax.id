“GIF
From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
For other uses, see GIF (disambiguation).
The Graphics Interchange Format (better known by its acronym GIF /ˈdʒɪf/ JIF or /ˈɡɪf/ GHIF) is a bitmap image format that was developed by American computer scientist Steve Wilhite while working at the bulletin board service (BBS) provider CompuServe on June 15, 1987.[1] It has since come into widespread usage on the World Wide Web due to its wide support and portability.
The format supports up to 8 bits per pixel for each image, allowing a single image to reference its own palette of up to 256 different colors chosen from the 24-bit RGB color space. It also supports animations and allows a separate palette of up to 256 colors for each frame. These palette limitations make GIF less suitable for reproducing color photographs and other images with color gradients, but it is well-suited for simpler images such as graphics or logos with solid areas of color.
GIF images are compressed using the Lempel–Ziv–Welch (LZW) lossless data compression technique to reduce the file size without degrading the visual quality. This compression technique was patented in 1985. Controversy over the licensing agreement between the software patent holder, Unisys, and CompuServe in 1994 spurred the development of the Portable Network Graphics (PNG) standard. By 2004 all the relevant patents had expired. (English, https://goo.gl/MUKjye).

(2) “Cara mengirim GIF ke teman dan grup Anda di WhatsApp
Anda dapat mengirim dan menerima GIF dan mengubah video menjadi GIF pada WhatsApp
Oleh VICTORIA WOOLLASTON
Sabtu 25 Maret 2017
Dibandingkan dengan aplikasi olahpesan lainnya, WhatsApp lambat membiarkan satu miliar pelanggannya mengirim GIF satu sama lain.
Pada bulan November 2016, perusahaan milik Facebook mengizinkan semua orang di iOS untuk mengirim dan mencari format file favorit internet – penyertaan ini setelah penambahan GIF untuk pengguna Android .
Beberapa hari kemudian, aplikasi menambahkan fungsi untuk mencari dan mengirimkannya ke iOS. Untuk melakukannya, Anda harus menjalankan setidaknya versi 2.16.16 aplikasi iOS. Sebelum fungsi pencarian ditambahkan versi 2.16.15 dari aplikasi perpesanan termasuk kemampuan untuk mengirim dan menerima GIF animasi, mengubah video Anda menjadi GIF (selama klip itu enam detik atau lebih pendek) dan mengirim Foto Langsung sebagai GIF animasi.
Sekarang WhatsApp meningkatkan penawaran GIF dengan membiarkan pengguna menelusuri dan mengirim GIF dari Giphy di Android.
Cara mencari GIF di Android
Dengan memanfaatkan kekuatan Giphy , pengguna Android dapat mengirim berbagai macam gambar bergerak. Aplikasi Android app v2.17.6 memungkinkan mereka yang menjalankan pencarian sistem operasi dan mengirim GIF dari arsip situs web. Perlu dicatat bahwa fiturnya dalam versi beta dan tidak jelas kapan akan diluncurkan lebih luas. Akibatnya, beberapa pengguna mungkin belum bisa melihat alatnya.
Menurut The Next Web jika Anda menekan tombol emoji di sebelah area inpyt teks di Android dan jentikkan ke bagian GIF, pencarian akan tersedia.
Cara mengirim GIF pada WhatsApp
Untuk mencari, lalu mengirim, GIF, klik tombol ‘+’, pilih Photo & Video Library dan rol kamera Anda akan muncul. Sebagai bagian dari update terbaru, kaca pembesar kecil sekarang ditampilkan di pojok kiri bawah dengan kata ‘GIF’. Pilih baris ini dan baris GIF akan muncul. Anda bisa browsing, atau mencari animasi tertentu.
Sebagai alternatif, Anda dapat menyimpan GIF ke handset Anda, walaupun menyimpan langsung dari situs Giphy, misalnya, default untuk menyimpan GIF sebagai gambar statis.
Jika Anda menginstal aplikasi Giphy Anda juga dapat mencari GIF yang Anda inginkan dengan reaksi (Happy), orang atau karakter (Alan Partridge) atau show (Seinfeld). Atau Anda dapat menjelajahi GIF dan topik populer.
Pilih GIF yang ingin Anda kirim dan di bawahnya Anda akan melihat deretan ikon termasuk SMS, email, Messenger, Facebook, Twitter, Instagram dan Pinterest. Tekan tiga titik pada akhir deretan ikon ini untuk melihat link dan berbagi ikon.
Klik Bagikan, pilih WhatsApp, pilih penerima dan tekan kirim.
Cara mengubah video menjadi GIF di WhatsApp
Buka obrolan WhatsApp yang ingin Anda gunakan untuk mengirim GIF.
Klik tanda ‘+’ di sudut kiri bawah dan masuk ke Photo & Video Library dan pilih video.
Potong klip dengan panjang yang Anda inginkan, anotasikan, doodle atau potret video menggunakan alat di pojok kanan atas dan kemudian matikan ikon biru ke GIF. Ini akan menghasilkan GIF Anda dan Anda dapat mengirimkannya dengan cara tradisional.
Jika Anda mengaturnya sehingga foto dan video bersama di WhatsApp disimpan ke gulungan kamera Anda, setiap GIF yang Anda buat dan bagikan akan disimpan. Sebagai alternatif, Anda dapat menekan terus GIF bersama untuk menyimpannya ke telepon Anda. Anda juga dapat meneruskan berbagi GIF ke kontak WhatsApp lainnya dari menu ini.

“How to send GIFs to your friends and groups on WhatsApp
You can send and receive GIFs and turn your videos into GIFs on WhatsApp
By VICTORIA WOOLLASTON
Saturday 25 March 2017
Compared to other messaging apps, WhatsApp was slow to allow its one billion customers to send GIFs to each other.
In November 2016, the Facebook-owned company allowed everyone on iOS to send and search for the internet’s favourite file format – this inclusion was after the addition of GIFs for Android users.
Just days later, the app added the function to search as well as send them on iOS. To do so, you need to be running at least version 2.16.16 of the iOS app. Before the search function was added version 2.16.15 of the messaging app included the ability to send and receive animated GIFs, turn your videos into GIFs (as long as the clip was six seconds or shorter) and send Live Photos as animated GIFs.
Now WhatsApp is improving the GIF offering by letting its users search through and send GIFs from Giphy on Android.
How to search for GIFs on Android
Leveraging the power of Giphy, Android users are able to send a huge variety of moving images. The v2.17.6 beta of the Android app lets those running the operating system search and send GIFs from the website’s archives. It should be noted that the feature is in beta and it is unclear when it will roll out more widely. As a result, some users may not yet be able to see the tool.
According to The Next Web if you press the emoji button next to the text inpyt area on Android and flick to the GIF section the search will be available.
How to send GIFs on WhatsApp
To search for, and then send, a GIF, click the ‘+’ button, select Photo & Video Library and your camera roll should appear. As part of the latest update, a small magnifying glass is now shown in the bottom left-hand corner with the word ‘GIF.’ Select this and rows of GIFs will appear. You can either browse, or search for a particular animation.
Alternatively you can save GIFs to your handset, although saving directly from the Giphy website, for example, defaults to saving the GIF as a static image.
If you install the Giphy app you can also search for the GIF you want by reaction (Happy), person or character (Alan Partridge) or show (Seinfeld). Or you can browse popular GIFs and topics.
Select the GIF you want to send and below it you’ll see a row of icons including SMS, email, Messenger, Facebook, Twitter, Instagram and Pinterest. Press the three dots at the end of this row of icons to see the link and share icons.
Click Share, select WhatsApp, choose your recipient and press send.
How to turn a video into a GIF on WhatsApp
Open the WhatsApp chat you want to use to send the GIF.
Click the ‘+’ sign in the bottom left-hand corner and go to Photo & Video Library and select a video.
Trim the clip to the length you want, annotate, doodle or crop the video using the tools in the top right-hand corner and then toggle the blue icon to GIF. This will generate your GIF and you can send it in the traditional way.
If you’be set it so that shared photos and videos on WhatsApp are saved to your camera roll, any GIF you create and share will be stored. Alternatively you can press and hold a shared GIF to save it to your phone. You can also forward shared GIFs to other WhatsApp contacts from this menu.
Facebook added GIF support in May last year, with Twitter following suit in February.” (English, https://goo.gl/W26Jga).
